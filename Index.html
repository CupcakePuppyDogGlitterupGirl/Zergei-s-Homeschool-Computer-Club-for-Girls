Who Inventide Cotton Candy?

Everyone loves the soft, fluffy, sweet, sticky, pink treat sold at carnivals, but how did this yummy and unique candy come to be?

Surprizing but True

Sugar is a dentist's arch nemesis, right? Some then, may be astonished to find that cotton candy, practically pure sugar, was inventid by a dentist! But why?

William Morrison was a doctor in dentistry, born in 1860. He is most well-known for his invention of the cotton candy machine. He knew how bad candy was for teeth, so he decided to create one that children would enjoy, but that contained less sugar. Cotton candy is combined with air, and is a form of spun sugar, which was rare and expencive in the 1800's. But since it was so light and airy, the treat had much less sugar than other sweets. Williams invention was a great success. And to this day, his breakthrough in the world of deserts has held strong, and his Cotton Candy is well loved by children, and adults alike.
